Overview

A clear first step with shared language and guardrails

Artificial intelligence is becoming part of everyday work, yet many organizations are unsure how to engage with it responsibly. The AI Readiness Snapshot helps your team understand what AI is, how it is showing up, and what safe and responsible use can look like in your context.

You will leave with practical guardrails and leadership level insight, so your organization can take thoughtful first steps with AI.

Who This Is For

Mission driven teams that want clarity without technical complexity

Designed for

  • Mission driven organizations, nonprofits, churches, and small teams
  • Leaders who want clarity about AI without technical complexity
  • Teams that value discernment, stewardship, and thoughtful decision making

No prior AI experience required

This is built for teams that are curious, cautious, or confused. You do not need an AI program, an engineering team, or an existing strategy to benefit.

What You Will Gain

Confidence, clarity, and realistic next questions

By the end, your team will

  • Understand what AI is and what it is not
  • Reduce fear and confusion around AI tools
  • Identify appropriate, low risk ways AI may support your work
  • Surface leadership questions to consider before deeper adoption

This is an orientation and learning experience, not a commitment to implementation.

What this protects you from

Reactive adoption, unclear expectations, tool sprawl, and avoidable risk. Most teams do not need a big AI program to start. They need shared language, sensible boundaries, and a calm first step.

What Is Included

Three components, one focused outcome

Pre Session Readiness Survey

Before the live session, participants complete a short survey to assess current understanding, concerns, and areas of curiosity related to AI. Results are used to tailor the session to your organization’s context and needs.

Live AI Readiness Session (90 minutes, virtual)

  • Plain language overview of AI and how it shows up in everyday work
  • Common myths and misunderstandings that create fear or misuse
  • Three practical principles for safe and responsible use
  • Two to three examples relevant to your organization’s work
  • Time for questions, reflection, and discussion

Accessible, interactive, and grounded in real world scenarios.

AI Use Snapshot (2 to 3 pages, delivered after the session)

Following the session, your organization receives a short customized summary that includes:

  • Where AI may be helpful for your team today
  • Key risks and guardrails leaders should be aware of
  • Questions leadership should consider before moving further
  • Clear boundaries around what has and has not been addressed

This gives leaders a concrete reference point for future conversations and decisions.

Scope Guardrails

Focused and low risk by design

To keep the scope tight and the risk low, this offering does not include:

  • Formal AI policy development
  • Organization wide training programs
  • Legal or compliance review
  • Tool selection, automation, or workflow implementation
  • Anything open ended or hourly billed
  • Pressure to move faster than you are ready

These areas can be explored separately if and when your organization is ready.
